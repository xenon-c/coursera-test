Visit With The Client

Notes

  • Most clients have no idea what they want in a website
  • It's YOUR JOB to ask questions to figure it out
  • Bring website examples of similar businesses
  • Encourage client to follow the rule of LESS (info) IS MORE - if everything's important, nothing's important
    • Encourage client not to cram info on the site
    • Help client identify the most crucial information and place it
  • Find a way for the client ot INVEST in the project
    • Especially true if you're doing this for free just to build up your portfolio
    • E.g. have them commit to pay for product photography
    • E.g. have them provide product for demo etc
    • Have them spend something (at least a bit of an investment) so they take it more seriously
  • Have client designated ONE PERSON responsible for decisions
  • Limit number of revisions UPFRONT (have them sign number of revisions you're willing to make)
    • If it's a paying job, limit number of FREE revisions (E.g. up to 3 times free, then charge per hour)
  • Google for "web development client questionnaire"
  • Involve others if needed
    • E.g. contact a local college and see if a graphic design student wants to join you by providing free design services (same for other aspects e.g. photography)
  • Get an idea of what the client has right now
